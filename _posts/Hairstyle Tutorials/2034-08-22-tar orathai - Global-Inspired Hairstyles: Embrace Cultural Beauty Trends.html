Hairstyles are a beautiful way to express cultural heritage and embrace global beauty trends. From the intricate braids of Africa to the elegant updos of Japan, each culture offers unique and inspiring hairstyles that you can incorporate into your daily look. In this article, we’ll explore a variety of global-inspired hairstyles that are not only fashionable but also celebrate the diverse beauty traditions from around the world.

1. African Tribal Braids

African tribal braids are known for their intricate patterns and stunning designs. These braids not only serve as a stylish choice but also have cultural significance, representing various tribes and traditions.

How to Create:

  1. Start by detangling and moisturizing your hair to prepare it for braiding.
  2. Part your hair into sections according to the braid pattern you desire—whether it’s cornrows, box braids, or twists.
  3. Begin braiding each section tightly, ensuring that the braids are consistent in size and neatness.
  4. Secure the ends with small elastics and, if desired, add decorative beads or threads to enhance the tribal aesthetic.
  5. Finish with a light oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz.

Best for:

  • Celebrating cultural heritage with intricate and artistic braid patterns.
  • Medium to long hair, ideal for showcasing the detailed braid designs.

2. Japanese Geisha Hair Updo

1. Gather Your Tools

Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary tools:

  • Eyeliner: Choose a high-quality liquid or gel eyeliner for a bold and defined line. Pen eyeliners are also convenient for precise application.
  • Eye Primer: Use an eye primer to create a smooth base and enhance the staying power of your eyeliner.
  • Brushes and Tools: A fine-tipped brush or an angled brush can help perfect the wing shape. Cotton swabs and makeup remover are useful for corrections.
  • Mirror: A magnifying mirror can provide a closer view for detailed application.

2. Prepare Your Eyelids

Proper preparation ensures a smooth application and long-lasting results:

  • Clean Your Eyelids: Start with clean, dry eyelids. Use a gentle cleanser to remove any residual makeup or oils.
  • Apply Eye Primer: Apply an eye primer evenly across your eyelids and let it dry. This will help your eyeliner adhere better and prevent smudging.
  • Set with Powder: Lightly dust a translucent powder over the primer to create a matte surface, which can help in achieving a smoother application.

3. Draw the Wing

Creating the perfect wing requires precision and technique:

  1. Define the Angle: Using a pencil or light eyeshadow, mark where you want the wing to end. This is usually angled upwards towards the end of your eyebrow.
  2. Start from the Outer Corner: Begin drawing the wing from the outer corner of your eye. Extend the line outward, following the angle you’ve defined. Use short, controlled strokes for accuracy.
  3. Connect the Wing: Connect the end of the wing to the middle of your upper lash line. This will form the base of your winged shape. Ensure that the line is smooth and even.

The traditional Japanese geisha hairstyle is characterized by its elegant, sophisticated updo and decorative hairpieces. This style is perfect for formal events and special occasions, offering a glimpse into historical beauty practices.

How to Create:

  1. Begin by smoothing and brushing your hair to remove tangles.
  2. Create a low bun at the nape of your neck, using bobby pins to secure it tightly.
  3. Pin sections of hair around the bun to form an elaborate, rounded shape, characteristic of the geisha look.
  4. Decorate with traditional hairpieces such as kanzashi (ornamental hairpins) or faux flowers for an authentic touch.
  5. Finish with a mist of strong-hold hairspray to keep the style in place.

Best for:

  • Formal events or themed parties where an elegant and traditional look is desired.
  • Medium to long hair that can be easily styled into a sophisticated updo.

3. Indian Bollywood Curls

Indian Bollywood hairstyles are known for their glamorous, voluminous curls and intricate adornments. This look is perfect for adding a touch of glamour to your everyday style or for special celebrations.

How to Create:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair and apply a volumizing mousse for extra body.
  2. Create large, loose curls using a curling iron or a heatless method like braiding overnight.
  3. Comb through the curls with your fingers to loosen them and add volume.
  4. Adorn your hair with decorative clips or headbands for a Bollywood-inspired finish.
  5. Set the style with a flexible-hold hairspray to maintain bounce and volume.

Best for:

  • Adding a touch of glamour to your look for special occasions or festive events.
  • Medium to long hair, ideal for showcasing voluminous curls and decorative accessories.

4. Brazilian Beachy Waves

Brazilian beachy waves capture the essence of relaxed, sun-kissed hair. This style is perfect for a casual, effortlessly chic look that embodies the laid-back vibe of Brazilian beaches.

How to Create:

  1. Start with damp hair and apply a sea salt spray or texturizing spray to add texture.
  2. Twist sections of hair and pin them in place to dry naturally or use a diffuser to enhance the beachy waves.
  3. Once dry, gently tousle the hair with your fingers to separate the waves and add volume.
  4. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to maintain the texture without stiffness.

Best for:

  • Casual outings or days when you want a relaxed, beach-inspired look.
  • All hair lengths, particularly effective for medium to long hair.

5. French Twisted Bun

The French twisted bun is a refined and stylish hairstyle that combines classic elegance with modern sophistication. It’s perfect for both everyday wear and special occasions.

How to Create:

  1. Start with smooth, detangled hair and create a low ponytail.
  2. Divide the ponytail into two sections and twist them around each other to create a rope-like effect.
  3. Wrap the twisted sections around the base of the ponytail to form a bun and secure with bobby pins.
  4. Smooth any flyaways with a small amount of hair gel or hairspray.
  5. Optional: Add a decorative pin or comb for an extra touch of elegance.

Best for:

  • Formal events or professional settings where a polished look is required.
  • Medium to long hair, particularly effective for creating a refined and sophisticated style.

Conclusion

Exploring global-inspired hairstyles not only allows you to diversify your look but also connects you with beauty traditions from around the world. Whether you’re interested in the intricate braids of Africa or the elegant updos of Japan, these styles offer a unique way to celebrate cultural beauty while enhancing your personal style. Embrace these global trends and make them your own, adding a touch of international flair to your everyday look.
